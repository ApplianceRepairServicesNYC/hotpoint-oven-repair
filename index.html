Common Gas Oven Problems

Burner Won't Light

When gas burners click but don't ignite, the igniter electrode may be dirty, cracked, or misaligned. Food debris or moisture can prevent proper sparking. Clean the igniter and burner ports carefully. If problems persist, the spark module or igniter may need replacement.

Oven Not Heating

Gas ovens that won't heat typically have igniter problems. The glow bar igniter must reach sufficient temperature to open the gas safety valve. Weak igniters glow but don't get hot enough—replacement restores proper oven heating.

Uneven Oven Temperature

Hot spots or uneven cooking may indicate thermostat calibration issues, damaged door gaskets, or blocked burner tubes. We diagnose the specific cause and restore even heat distribution throughout the oven cavity.

Common Electric Oven Problems

Element Not Heating

When a coil element doesn't heat, try it in a different receptacle to verify whether the element or receptacle is faulty. Burned-out elements show visible damage. We replace elements and receptacles to restore proper heating.

Oven Temperature Inaccurate

Electric ovens that don't maintain set temperatures typically have thermostat issues. We test actual temperatures against settings and replace thermostats or sensors as needed for accurate temperature control.

Multiple Elements Not Working

When multiple elements fail simultaneously, check terminal block connections and wiring. Loose or burned connections commonly cause multiple element failures.
